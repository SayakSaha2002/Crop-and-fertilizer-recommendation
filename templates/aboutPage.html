CROPLIFE
DESCRIPTION

Agriculture in crops, commonly referred to as crop farming or arable farming, is a fundamental aspect of agriculture that involves cultivating plants for food, fiber, fuel, and other purposes. It plays a crucial role in feeding the world's population, providing raw materials for various industries, and supporting livelihoods in rural communities. Large-scale farming aimed at producing crops and livestock for sale and profit. Industrial Agriculture involves high levels of synthetic fertilizers and pesticides to maximize productivity.

CROPS

Food crops, include grains(wheat,rice,corn), vegetables, fruits, Cash crops such as tea, coffee, cotton, Industrial crops like rubber and sugarcane, Oil crops include oil seeds like soybean, sunflower and other biofuels.

FERTILIZER INNOVATION
DESCRIPTION

Fertilizers play a critical role in modern agriculture by supplying essential nutrients to crops, improving soil fertility, and increasing yields. They help replace nutrients that crops remove from the soil and address specific nutrient deficiencies, making them a cornerstone of crop production worldwide. Types of Fertilizers in Agriculture: Organic Fertilizers (Derived from natural sources such as plant and animal matter.) Inorganic (Synthetic) Fertilizers (Manufactured through industrial processes, providing precise concentrations of nutrients).

ESSENTIAL NUTRIENTS

Macronutrients (Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K)), Secondary nutrients (Calcium (Ca), Magnesium (Mg), Sulfur (S)), Micronutrients (Iron (Fe), Zinc (Zn), Copper (Cu))
